Terms of Service · Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Last updated 10 July 2026

Who runs Glossary

Glossary is a personal reading-companion app operated by an individual, not a company. For anything in this policy, contact iloveexo159@gmail.com.

What is collected, and when

Only if you sign in. Signing in with Google shares your name, email address and profile photo with the app, and creates an account identifier. Your account then stores what you make: flashcards (with your notes, tags and highlights), a summary of each finished review session, and a log of your exports.

Without signing in, nothing leaves your device. Cards, preferences, recent searches and cached articles stay in this browser's local storage. Preferences and recent searches stay local even when signed in.

Where it lives

Account data is stored in Google Firebase (Authentication and Cloud Firestore), processed by Google LLC, and may be held on servers outside your country. Each account's data is isolated by security rules — one account can never read another's.

What it's used for

One thing: syncing your own content across your own devices. Glossary has no ads, no analytics or tracking, and never sells or shares your data with anyone.

Retention and deletion

Your data is kept until you remove it. Deleting a card removes it everywhere. Account → Delete account & data permanently erases your entire account — cards, review history, export log and the sign-in itself — immediately and irreversibly. You can also email the address above to request removal.

Your rights

Consistent with Singapore's PDPA and the GDPR: you can see everything the app holds (it's all in the app), correct it (edit any card), take it with you (Settings → Export JSON), and erase it (above) — no request forms, no waiting.

Changes

If this policy changes materially, the date above changes and the login page will say so.

Terms of Service

Last updated 10 July 2026

The service

Glossary is a free, personal reading tool provided as-is, with no uptime or data-durability guarantees. Keep your own backups of anything important (Settings → Export JSON).

Your content

Your notes, tags and highlights are yours. Signing in grants the app only what it needs to store and sync them for you — nothing more.

Third-party content

Article summaries come from Wikipedia and are available under CC BY-SA; dictionary definitions come from the Free Dictionary API and synonyms from Datamuse. Their content remains subject to their licenses.

Fair use of a shared, free service

The app runs on a free storage tier shared by everyone. Automated scraping, bulk writes, or attempts to access another account's data may result in the account being removed.

Ending things

You can delete your account at any time (Account → Delete account & data). The operator may remove accounts that abuse the service, and will delete the account's data in doing so.

Governing law

These terms are governed by the laws of Singapore.
